About

I am a Ph.D. student in Water Science and Management at New Mexico State University, with prior professional doctoral training in economic development.

My primary research interests are in environmental and natural resource economics. My secondary interests include applied microeconomics and health economics. I am particularly interested in using empirical methods to understand how institutions, regulations, and governance influence natural resource, environmental, and health outcomes.

Teaching

I approach teaching with the conviction that students deserve clarity, structure, and a space where they can ask questions freely. I help students connect concepts, practise analytical tools, and build confidence across a range of economics courses at New Mexico State University and the University of New Mexico. My teaching philosophy is grounded in trust, respect, and confidentiality between instructors and students.
