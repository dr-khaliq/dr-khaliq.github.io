My research interests lie at the intersection of applied microeconomics and environmental and natural resource economics. As a first-semester PhD student in Water Economics and Policy, I draw on prior work in energy and environmental policy, resource governance, and institutional analysis. I am particularly interested in energy, water, and environmental policy, with a focus on how institutions and regulation shape resource use and environmental outcomes.

Teaching Experience

I approach teaching with the mindset that students deserve clarity, structure, and a space where they can ask questions freely. I enjoy helping them connect concepts, practice tools, and build confidence across a wide range of economics courses at both New Mexico State University and the University of New Mexico.

My teaching philosophy has also been shaped by experiences that highlighted the importance of trust, respect, and confidentiality between students and instructors. I ensure that students feel safe, respected, and supported when they share their concerns or personal challenges.
